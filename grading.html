The four condition grades

Pick the grade that honestly matches your copy. Sealed and Complete earn the most; loose discs still sell, and we don't buy non-working or counterfeit copies.

Brand New (Sealed)

Factory sealed and never opened, in the original manufacturer shrink wrap.

  • Original, unbroken factory seal — tight, with no cuts, reseals, or replacement wrap
  • Correct seal style and any factory stickers for that title and region
  • Not for "resealed," store security wrap, or opened-then-rewrapped copies — grade those as Complete
We inspect the seal closely. If a sealed copy turns out to be resealed, we re-grade it as Complete and explain why on your claim.

Complete (CIB)

Complete in box: the original case, cover art, any inserts, and a clean, working disc or cartridge.

  • Original case in good shape — intact hinges and clasps, no major cracks
  • Original cover art for the correct title, edition, and region
  • Disc with only light surface marks, or a cartridge with clean contacts — boots and plays fully
  • Manuals and inserts that originally shipped, where applicable
Minor wear is normal and fine. Deep scratches that affect play, heavy case damage, or missing art may move a copy toward Loose — we'll note exactly what we found.

Game Only (Loose)

The disc or cartridge only — no original case or artwork — but clean and fully working.

  • Genuine disc or cartridge that boots and plays start to finish
  • Readable label; clean contacts or only light, non-blocking disc scratches
  • Case and inserts not required — include them and it likely grades Complete instead
Pack loose discs and carts so they can't rattle or scratch in transit (paper sleeve or a spare case is perfect).

Not Working / Counterfeit

We buy authentic, working games only. These aren't eligible and are returned to you.

  • Won't boot, freezes, or won't play through — cracked discs or damaged cartridges
  • Counterfeit, reproduction, or pirated copies, or burned/copied discs
  • Region or title that doesn't match the case or the claim
If you're unsure whether a copy is genuine, submit it anyway — we verify authenticity on inspection and simply return anything we can't accept, free.

Authenticity check

Every game is verified as genuine before we make an offer. Honest sellers can confirm a real copy the same way:

  • Official markings. Genuine discs and cartridges carry the platform maker's logos, part numbers, and region codes, printed crisply and consistently.
  • Label quality. Real labels are sharp and correctly aligned — fakes often show fuzzy print, off colors, wrong fonts, or a glued-on paper label.
  • Cartridge build. Authentic carts use the correct shell, screws, and board; reproductions often differ in weight, color, or screw type.
  • Source. Copies bought new from major retailers are almost always genuine. Deep-discount "sealed" copies from unknown sellers are the most common fakes.

    • By submitting a claim you certify the games are authentic and yours to sell. We don't accept counterfeit or stolen property; see our Terms for details.

    Before you ship — your checklist

    A couple of minutes here keeps your final offer matching your estimate.

    • Match the grade. Confirm each game against the grades above and pick the one that honestly fits.
    • Include what counts. Claiming Complete? Pack the case, cover art, and inserts together.
    • Clean it up. Wipe discs with a soft cloth from center outward; gently clean cartridge contacts. Don't use harsh solvents.
    • Test it. Make sure each game boots and plays — condition and working order are confirmed on arrival.
    • Pack to protect. Wrap each game so nothing shifts or scratches. Use the prepaid label we email after we accept your claim.
    • Keep your tracking. You're responsible for the package until it reaches us, so hold onto the drop-off receipt.
